Careers

  • Who uses software
    • Everyone
  • You can get a job in any industry
  • If an industry doesn’t utilize software
    • Enter it as an entrepreneur!

Grad school

  • Get a job, or stay in school
  • If you can get the job you want right now
    • Take it
    • Few exceptions to this
  • Master’s degree
    • Extend you bachelors degree
  • PhD
    • Research

Different types of industry

  • Startups
  • Large corporations
  • Research
  • Academia

Internships

  • Sample an organization
    • Great if you’re not sure where you want to go
  • Shift to hiring through internships
    • Hire lots of interns
    • Poor performance
      • Gone in a few months
      • Not a big loss financially (opposed to hiring full-time)
    • Good performance
      • Full time offer (usually)

Expected Pay

  • Academia is more freedom and less chasing someone else’s bottom line
    • Makes bigger difference from any level

Professionalism

  • You have responsibilities
    • Take care of them
    • On time!
  • Communicate!
    • Clear
    • Concise
    • Often
    • Bring up issues / don’t over promise
  • Honesty
  • Don’t be a burden

Side Note:

Professionalism

  • Not to be confused with acting professional

Acting Professional

  • You’ve heard the tips before
  • You should fit into your companies culture
    • If your company acts professional
      • You should act professional
    • If you don’t like acting professional
      • Seek employment where you’re not expected to
      • How many CS professors wear suits?
